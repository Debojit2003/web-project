Butter Chicken

Butter chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is a popular Indian dish. Tender chicken is marinated in spiced yogurt, cooked in a tomato-based sauce with butter and cream. It's known for its rich, creamy texture and a blend of flavorful spices. Served with naan or rice, it's a beloved dish worldwide.

Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular Indian dish, featuring grilled, marinated chicken in a creamy tomato-based sauce. Key ingredients include yogurt, garam masala, and ginger-garlic paste for the marinade. The sauce incorporates crushed tomatoes, spices, and heavy cream. Served with rice or naan, it offers a rich and flavorful experience.

Chicken Dum Biryani

Chicken dum biryani is a mouthwatering Indian rice dish, where marinated chicken and fragrant basmati rice are layered and slow-cooked to perfection in a sealed pot (dum). The chicken is infused with aromatic spices, while the rice absorbs the flavors. The result is a delectable and richly flavored biryani, loved for its combination of tender chicken, fragrant rice, and enticing spices. It's a celebratory dish, often served at special occasions and gatherings, and a favorite among biryani enthusiasts worldwide.

Chana Masala

Chana Masala is a popular vegetarian Indian dish made from chickpeas (chana) cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce. The dish is seasoned with various spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala, creating a rich and aromatic flavor. It's often served with rice or flatbreads and is enjoyed for its hearty and satisfying taste.

Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a delicious Indian vegetarian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) and spinach (palak). The spinach is blanched and pureed, creating a vibrant green sauce. Paneer cubes are added to the spinach sauce along with spices like cumin, garam masala, and turmeric. The result is a creamy, flavorful, and nutritious dish typically served with rice or naan.

Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a classic Punjabi lentil dish with black gram dal (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma). Slow-cooked with onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices, it gains a rich and creamy texture. Finished with butter and cream, it offers a luscious and indulgent flavor. Served with naan or rice, it's a popular and comforting staple in Indian cuisine.
